III. Handling the "Big Three" Objections
Objection
The "Completion-Proof" Answer
"Why not just use Adobe?"
"Adobe is a heavy, expensive creative suite. DocumentFlow is a lightweight utility that does exactly what your team needs in two clicks, without the $300/year price tag."
"Is it secure?"
"100%. Unlike other web tools, DocumentFlow uses 'Zero-Server' logic. The document stays in the computer's RAM and vanishes when the tab is closed. No data is ever transmitted to me or any cloud."
"What if it breaks?"
"The tool is built on native Web Components. It has no server dependencies, so there’s no 'server' to go down. If you have a browser, it works."
IV. The "Pilot Program" Strategy
Don't ask for a $50,000 contract on day one. Ask for a Paid Pilot.
The Offer: "I'll give your team of 10 access for 30 days for a flat fee of $500."
The Goal: Collect data. Ask them: "How many files did you fix? How much time did you save?"
The Upsell: Use that data to sell the Phase 2 "Client-Facing Gateway" for a much higher price.
