ROI & Cost Analysis: The Business Case for DocumentFlow
Implementing the DocumentFlow Suite isn't just a technical upgrade; it is a direct intervention in the rising costs of administrative overhead and software licensing.
1. The "Adobe Tax" Reduction
Standardizing on DocumentFlow allows the organization to reclaim significant licensing costs. Most employees only require Acrobat Pro for basic triage (merging, rotating, and shrinking). DocumentFlow handles these tasks at a fraction of the cost.
Average Acrobat Pro License: ~$288 / user / year

Direct Savings: For a 100-person team, this represents a potential $28,800 annual reduction in software spend.
2. Labor Cost Recovery (Productivity Gains)
The "hidden" cost of manual document triage is the time employees spend fighting with incompatible file types.
The 15-Minute Rule:
If an employee saves just 15 minutes per week by not having to re-request files from clients or manually "clean" sideways photos:
Weekly Savings: 0.25 hours $\times$ $35/hr (Avg. Wage) = $8.75 / employee
Annual Savings: ~$455 / employee
Total Team Value (100 Users): $45,500 in reclaimed labor capacity.
3. The "NIGO" Impact (Reducing Re-Work)
"Not In Good Order" (NIGO) documents are the primary cause of processing delays in the retirement and financial sectors. When a document is rejected because it’s unreadable or sideways, the cost is doubled:
The Cost of Rejection: Staff time to call the client and explain the error.
The Cost of Re-Processing: Starting the workflow from scratch 3–5 days later.
DocumentFlow eliminates NIGO at the source by ensuring every file is upright, legible, and optimized before it is ever submitted.
