## 1. Purpose

This page is the "Decision Maker's Favorite." It translates technical efficiency into hard currency: licensing savings, labor recovery, and NIGO reduction.

Implementing the DocumentFlow Suite is a direct intervention in rising administrative overhead and software licensing costs.

## 2. The "Adobe Tax" reduction

Standardizing on DocumentFlow reclaims significant licensing costs. Many employees only need Acrobat Pro for basic triage (merging, rotating, shrinking). DocumentFlow handles these tasks at a fraction of the cost.

---

## 3. Labor cost recovery (productivity gains)

The hidden cost is time employees spend on incompatible file types.

**The 15-minute rule:** If an employee saves 15 minutes per week by not re-requesting files or manually cleaning sideways photos:

- **Weekly savings:** 0.25 hours × $35/hr (avg. wage) ≈ $8.75 / employee  
- **Annual savings:** ~$455 / employee  
- **Total (100 users):** ~$45,500 in reclaimed labor capacity  

---

## 4. The "NIGO" impact (reducing re-work)

"Not In Good Order" (NIGO) documents are a primary cause of processing delays in retirement and financial sectors. When a document is rejected (unreadable or sideways), cost is doubled:

- **Cost of rejection:** Staff time to call the client and explain the error.  
- **Cost of re-processing:** Restarting the workflow 3–5 days later.  

DocumentFlow eliminates NIGO at the source: every file is upright, legible, and optimized before submission.

## 6. Beyond the numbers (soft ROI)

- **Improved employee morale:** Staff focus on high-value tasks instead of "fixing photos."

- **Risk mitigation:** Less "Shadow IT" use of unsecure, free online PDF converters.
