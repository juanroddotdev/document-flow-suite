# Legal and IP Shield

Context document for DocumentFlow Suite: IP protection, entity formation, licensing, and outreach. Merged from Legal Shield for Side Projects.

---

## 1. Purpose

This is the "Sleep Well at Night" section: the least exciting but most important for ensuring the side project doesn't become the employer's property or a personal liability.

**Disclaimer:** This is a strategic framework based on common industry practice, not legal advice. Consult a legal or tax professional before signing official documents.

---

## 2. The "Day Job" audit (IP protection)

Before writing the first line of code, ensure the employment contract does not hand over ownership of this work.

- **Review the PIIA:** Check the Proprietary Information and Inventions Agreement. Look for "Assignment of Inventions" and similar terms.
- **Moonlighting rule:** Confirm the contract doesn't explicitly forbid side businesses in the same industry (e.g. Financial Services / FinTech).
- **Clean room strategy:**
  - NEVER use a company laptop for this project.
  - NEVER use company software licenses (e.g. office Adobe or GitHub account).
  - NEVER work on this during company hours or on company Wi-Fi.
- **Action item:** Keep a log of "Development Hours" to prove they occurred outside 9-to-5.

---

## 3. Entity formation (LLC)

To move from "project" to "business," separate personal assets from the software's liabilities.

- **Goal:** Create a Single-Member LLC (Limited Liability Company).
- **Why:** If a company claims the tool caused a data breach (even if it didn't), they sue the LLC, not you. Protects house, car, and personal savings.
- **Timeline:**
  - **Phase 1 (Development):** Operate as a "Project" (Sole Proprietorship).
  - **Phase 2 (Selling):** Form the LLC before signing the first contract with "Company B."

---

## 4. Licensing strategy

You are granting a **license to use**, not ownership of the code.

- **Commercial license:** "The software is provided 'As-Is' with no warranty." Standard for small providers to limit liability for a client's internal failures.
- **Open source dependencies:** pdf-lib and Lit (MIT/BSD) are safe for commercial use as long as original license notices are included (e.g. in an "About" section).

---

## 5. "Company B" outreach strategy

To avoid conflict with the current employer:

- **Transparency (optional):** If the relationship allows, ask for a **Letter of Non-Interest**—a document where the company states it has no claim to this specific PDF tool.
- **White-label pitch:** When selling to Company B, frame it as a standalone utility developed to solve a universal industry problem, not something taken from the current job.
