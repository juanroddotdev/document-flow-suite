# Sales and Outreach

Context document for DocumentFlow Suite: Phase 1 sales, elevator pitch, objections, and pilot. Merged from Productizing a Side Project.

---

## 1. Mindset

This is where the "Side Project" becomes a "Product." To sell effectively—especially to a company skeptical of a solo developer—stop talking about code and start talking about **Operations**. In Phase 1 you are not selling a "PDF Tool"; you are selling **Administrative Recovery**.

---

## 2. The elevator pitch (30-second hook)

> "We've noticed that about 30% of the documents our team receives from clients are 'Not In Good Order'—they're sideways, the wrong format (like HEIC), or massive files that crash our systems. Currently we're paying for Adobe Acrobat licenses just to fix these. I've developed a lightweight, browser-based utility that 'normalizes' these files instantly without the data ever leaving our secure network. It's designed to save about 15 minutes per document and eventually replace those high-cost licenses."

---

## 3. Company B outreach script (LinkedIn / email)

**Subject:** Solving the "HEIC/NIGO" bottleneck in [Department Name]

**Body:**

> Hi [Name],
>
> I've been working on a solution for a common friction point in the retirement/financial sector: the high cost and time waste of processing non-standard client documents.
>
> Most teams overpay for PDF software just to rotate images or merge files. I've built a 'Zero-Server' utility called DocumentFlow that handles this triage directly in the browser. It ensures HIPAA-level privacy because the data never hits a third-party server, and it's designed to normalize mobile photos into professional, 150-DPI PDFs.
>
> I'm looking for a partner company to run a small Phase 1 pilot with their internal team. Would you be open to a 10-minute demo to see how it could reduce your Adobe licensing spend?

---

## 4. The "big three" objections

| Objection | Completion-proof answer |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| **"Why not just use Adobe?"** | "Adobe is a heavy, expensive creative suite. DocumentFlow is a lightweight utility that does exactly what your team needs in two clicks, without the $300/year price tag." |
| **"Is it secure?"** | "100%. Unlike other web tools, DocumentFlow uses 'Zero-Server' logic. The document stays in the computer's RAM and vanishes when the tab is closed. No data is ever transmitted to me or any cloud." |
| **"What if it breaks?"** | "The tool is built on native Web Components. It has no server dependencies, so there's no 'server' to go down. If you have a browser, it works." |

---

## 5. Pilot program strategy

- **Don't ask for a $50,000 contract on day one.** Ask for a **Paid Pilot**.
- **Offer:** "I'll give your team of 10 access for 30 days for a flat fee of $500."
- **Goal:** Collect data. Ask: "How many files did you fix? How much time did you save?"
- **Upsell:** Use that data to sell the Phase 2 Client-Facing Gateway at a higher price.
